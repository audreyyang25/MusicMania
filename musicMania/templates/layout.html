A Brief History of Classical Music

Classical music is generally divided into four musical periods: Baroque, Classical, Romantic, and Contemporary. The following sections will give a brief overview of each era.

Romantic Period (1800-1910)

The Romantic period is generally characterized by the intensity of energy and passion in musical compositions. Composers during this time focused more on nature, national identity, and the supernatural. Nocturnes, etudes, and rhapsodies became more popular, in addition to traditional structures like sonatas. In addition, composers experimented with new musical conventions and formats, using a wider range of dynamics and pitch, as well as more liberty during performances.

Contemporary Period (1890 - present)

The Contemporary Period can be subdivided into the Modernist period (1890 - 1975) and the Contemporary period (1950 - present). The Modernist period includes the Impressionist, Expressionist, and Neoclassical movements, which represent a transition from Romantic to Contemporary styles. Impressionism was an especially notable musical movement that focused on using more unique "colors" and tonal combinations; it is often associated with composers Debussy and Ravel. The Contemporary period is notable for its rejection of traditionally accepted composition techniques and styles, and its exploratory harmonies.
