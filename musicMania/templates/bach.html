Johann Sebastian Bach (1685-1750) was a German composer and musician of the Baroque period, widely regarded as one of the greatest composers in the history of Western classical music. Born into a musical family, Bach's early career involved various positions as a court musician and organist. His music is characterized by intricate counterpoint, harmonic sophistication, and a profound understanding of musical structure. Bach's contributions to music are immense, particularly in the development of the fugue and the exploration of complex polyphony. His works encompass a wide range of genres, including sacred and secular music, vocal and instrumental compositions. Some of his most famous pieces include the Brandenburg Concertos, the Mass in B Minor, the Goldberg Variations, and the Well-Tempered Clavier. Bach's compositions have had a lasting impact on the development of Western classical music, and his legacy continues to be celebrated and studied worldwide.
